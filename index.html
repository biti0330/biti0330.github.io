ViaCar

The ViaCar competition is hosted by IEEE and sponsored by ViaSat.

Discription about competition: The competition is hosted in 2014 at University of California, San Diego. Teams from each schools (such as UCSD, UCLA, CSU Fulleton...) gathered and competed with each other.

Discription about our design: Because years after years, every teams always sticked with the design of using magnetism and camera to be the sensors for their cars. Therefore, we chose to be different, and our sensor was based on the light emitting from LED, and I am responsible for that. The design for the sensor was simple by using Inverter Op-amp and low-pass filter.

How the our car work: The car turn left, right and go straight will be based on the voltage from reflected light. The allogrithm was if the input voltage of the left is higher than both straight and right, the car need to turn right in order to not go out of track. If the voltage at the middle is higher than left or right, the car should go straight.


Even though we was not on the top 3 but our car finished the track. We also know that using LED with a lot of restrictions from the competition will limit the responsive of our car. However, I learned many things from the project.

FACEMAP

A Social App by Tailor Swift

Discription of the App: The purpose of the app is that users can share and encourage other users' feelings without afraid of being judged.

Discription about our design: The language that we used was Swift to design for our app. We also used Multipeer Connectivity and FireBase database and Apple Map in our design.

How the app works: First, at the login page, user can create a temporary nickname which will be deleted if they quit the app. Then, they can chose their moods and the interests that they would like to talk about. Those interests and moods and their nicknames will be display on the map, where other users can see and send a chat request if they want to have a conversation. The app also has two functions which are On Grid (internet or data required) and Off Grid (offline mode using multipeer connectivity). Therefore, users can use either when they have internet or when they do not have internet.
